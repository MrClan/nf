Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a typical ethics review take?▾
Timelines depend on risk level: expedited reviews are faster; full-board reviews follow scheduled meetings. Ensure submissions are complete to avoid delays.
What documents are required?▾
Protocol, consent/assent forms, participant materials, data management and safety plans, recruitment content, and investigator CVs.
When is continuing review needed?▾
Annual continuing review (or per approval conditions) plus prompt reporting of serious adverse events and protocol deviations.
How is data protection handled?▾
Apply data minimization, secure storage, controlled access, and de-identification where possible; describe protections in the protocol.
Do community studies require special considerations?▾
Engage community leaders, use culturally appropriate consent, and address local risk/benefit and feedback mechanisms.
